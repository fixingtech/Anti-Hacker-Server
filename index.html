How You Can Be Attacked

1. Phishing Scams

Attackers send fraudulent emails or messages disguised as trusted entities (like your bank or a company). They trick you into clicking a malicious link or entering personal credentials on a fake website. Always verify the sender's actual email address.

2. Malware & Viruses

Malicious software (like viruses, trojans, or spyware) can infect your PC through fake downloads or infected USB drives. Ransomware is a type of malware that locks your files until a ransom is paid. Keep your OS and antivirus updated!

3. Weak Passwords

If you use simple passwords (like "123456" or "password") or reuse the same password across multiple sites, hackers can easily guess them or use data from one site breach to access all your accounts. Use a password manager and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Simple Steps to Get Secured

  1. 1

    Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

    Turn on two-factor authentication for all critical accounts (email, bank, social media). This makes it almost impossible for a hacker to log in, even if they have your password.

  2. 2

    Keep Your Software Updated

    Regularly update your operating system (Windows, macOS), browser, and antivirus. Updates often include critical security patches that fix known vulnerabilities.

  3. 3

    Backup Your Data Regularly

    Store important files on an external drive or cloud service. This ensures you can recover your data quickly if your PC is hit by ransomware or a hardware failure.

  4. 4

    Be Skeptical of Links and Attachments

    If an email seems suspicious, do not click links or download attachments. Instead, navigate directly to the company's official website in your browser.
